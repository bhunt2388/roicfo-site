The Inflation Reduction Act created IRC §6418, allowing carbon capture project owners to transfer their Section 45Q federal tax credits to unrelated third-party taxpayers for cash. The buyer receives a dollar-for-dollar federal income tax offset. No equity. No K-1. No project ownership. No passive activity limitations. Clean statutory transfer — applied directly to the buyer's 2025 federal income tax return.

IRC §170 · §6418
Leveraged Donation Tax Strategy
"A 5:1 charitable deduction on every eligible contribution."
Deduction Ratio 5 : 1
Net Benefit / $1M Income ~$91,000
Minimum Investment $50,000
Carryforward Period 5 Years
Listed Transaction Risk None — Not a Listed Transaction

IRC §170 permits charitable deductions at fair market value — not just cost basis. Through a qualified partnership structure, taxpayer-partners acquire allocated mineral interests at a fraction of appraised fair market value, then donate those interests to a qualified charity at full FMV. The result: a federally compliant 5:1 deduction ratio, backed by a qualified appraisal signed by both appraiser and charity officer. K-1, Form 8283, and full tax return documentation are delivered to the advisor and client.

Compliance Framework
Not a listed transaction under IRS Rev. Proc. 2004-65 — no disclosure burden
IRS "tacking" rules provide long-term holding period on charitable asset from day one
Donation to a Native American qualified charity with active 501(c)(3) determination
Form 8283 co-signed by qualified appraiser and donee charity officer
Partnership K-1 and full supporting documentation delivered for tax return filing
